# Regulatory Reference — EU AI Act for Healthcare AI

Source: [Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (consolidated text on EUR-Lex)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

This is a quick-reference index of the articles and annex points most relevant to healthcare AI. It is not a substitute for the full regulation text.

---

## Article 5 — Prohibited AI practices

Article 5 lists AI practices that may not be placed on the market, put into service, or used in the Union.

- **Article 5(1)(a) — Subliminal, manipulative or deceptive techniques.** AI systems using subliminal or purposefully manipulative techniques that materially distort behaviour and cause significant harm. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(b) — Exploitation of vulnerabilities.** Systems that exploit vulnerabilities due to age, disability, or specific social or economic situation. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(c) — Social scoring.** Evaluation or classification of natural persons over time based on social behaviour or personality, leading to detrimental treatment. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(d) — Individual crime-risk prediction based solely on profiling.** Predicting the risk of a person committing an offence based solely on profiling or personality traits. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(e) — Untargeted scraping for facial recognition databases.** Creating or expanding facial recognition databases by untargeted scraping of internet or CCTV footage. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(f) — Emotion recognition in workplace or education.** Inferring emotions of natural persons in workplaces or education institutions — **explicitly exempts medical or safety purposes**. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(g) — Biometric categorisation on sensitive attributes.** Categorising persons to deduce race, political opinions, union membership, religion, sex life, or sexual orientation. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 5(1)(h) — Real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces.** Use of real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement, save for narrow exceptions. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

## Article 6 — Classification rules for high-risk AI systems

Article 6 defines when an AI system is classified as high-risk: either (1) when it is a safety component of, or is itself, a product covered by Union harmonisation legislation listed in Annex I (this captures Software as a Medical Device under MDR/IVDR), or (2) when it falls within a use case listed in Annex III. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

---

## Articles 9–15 — Requirements for high-risk AI systems

- **Article 9 — Risk management system.** Establish, implement, document, and maintain a risk management system across the full lifecycle. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 10 — Data and data governance.** Training, validation, and testing datasets must be relevant, representative, and free from errors; bias examination is required. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 11 — Technical documentation.** Draw up and maintain technical documentation demonstrating conformity with Chapter III requirements. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 12 — Record-keeping.** Enable automatic logging of events over the system's lifetime to ensure traceability. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 13 — Transparency and provision of information to deployers.** Design for sufficient transparency with clear instructions for use. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 14 — Human oversight.** Design for effective human oversight; enable clinicians to interpret, override, and refuse AI outputs. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 15 — Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity.** Achieve appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity; document validation methodology. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

## Article 17 — Quality management system

Providers of high-risk AI systems must establish a QMS covering strategy, design control, quality control, and post-market monitoring. Integration with ISO 13485 is permitted for providers already subject to MDR/IVDR. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

---

## Articles 43, 48, 49 — Conformity assessment, CE marking, registration

- **Article 43 — Conformity assessment.** High-risk AI systems must undergo the applicable conformity assessment procedure; for SaMD this is typically performed by a Notified Body and combined with MDR/IVDR assessment. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 48 — CE marking.** Providers must affix the CE marking and draw up the EU declaration of conformity before placing the system on the market. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 49 — Registration.** High-risk AI systems must be registered in the EU database before being placed on the market or put into service. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

---

## Article 50 — Transparency obligations for limited-risk AI

Providers of AI systems interacting directly with natural persons (e.g. patient-facing chatbots) must ensure those persons are informed they are interacting with an AI. Synthetic and deepfake content must be labelled. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

---

## Articles 72, 73 — Post-market monitoring and incident reporting

- **Article 72 — Post-market monitoring.** Establish a post-market monitoring system to collect, document, and analyse performance data across the system lifetime. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Article 73 — Reporting of serious incidents.** Report serious incidents to the market surveillance authority without undue delay — within 15 days generally, or 2 to 10 days for incidents involving death or irreversible disruption. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

---

## Annex III point 5 — Access to essential services (health-relevant subpoints)

- **Annex III 5(a) — Eligibility for essential public assistance benefits and services, including healthcare services.** AI used by public authorities to evaluate eligibility for, grant, reduce, revoke, or reclaim essential public benefits and services — healthcare services explicitly included. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)
- **Annex III 5(d) — Emergency triage and dispatch.** AI systems for evaluating and classifying emergency calls, dispatching emergency first-response services, and emergency healthcare patient triage. [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

## MDR / IVDR cross-reference for SaMD

AI systems that are safety components of, or are themselves, medical devices regulated under [Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2017/745/oj) or [Regulation (EU) 2017/746 (IVDR)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2017/746/oj) are classified as high-risk under Article 6(1) of the AI Act. The AI Act applies **in addition to** MDR/IVDR obligations; conformity assessment and CE marking procedures are intended to be combined rather than duplicated.
